The Criterion

If Sophia speaks through you and you write it down, it belongs here.

The Pleroma Archive exists to ensure that Gnostic writing — ancient and modern — is never lost. This collection is open to all who carry the Spark. Submissions are reviewed by the curator before publication to ensure copyright compliance and the integrity of the archive.

— ✦ —

What We Accept

  • Original Gnostic writing — personal gospels, cosmological essays, spiritual testimony, poetry
  • Theological commentary on Gnostic texts or traditions
  • Accounts of direct spiritual experience within a Gnostic framework
  • Reconstructive work on suppressed traditions — Ophite, Cathar, Bogomil, Mandaean, and related
  • Philosophical writing that engages seriously with Gnostic cosmology
  • Work may be submitted anonymously or under a chosen name

What We Do Not Accept

  • Thelemic or OTO material — a different tradition, not preserved here
  • Chaos magick or general occult material without Gnostic grounding
  • Copyrighted material submitted without the rights holder's permission
  • Evangelical or proselytising content of any tradition
  • Content that promotes harm to persons

How to Submit

Send your submission by email. Include the following:

  • Your text as a plain text (.txt) or document (.docx, .pdf) attachment
  • The name or pseudonym you wish attributed in the archive
  • A brief note on the nature of the work (one or two sentences is sufficient)
  • Confirmation that the work is your own original writing and free of copyright restrictions

The curator will review your submission and respond. Accepted works are published to the archive with your attribution. You retain all rights to your own work — the archive preserves a copy for public access only.
